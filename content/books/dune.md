# Notes on Dune

## Key Themes
- Politics and power structures
- Ecology and environmental adaptation
- Religion as a tool of control
- The hero's journey subverted

## Favorite Quotes
> "I must not fear. Fear is the mind-killer."

> "Deep in the human unconscious is a pervasive need for a logical universe that makes sense. But the real universe is always one step beyond logic."

## Thoughts
One of the greatest science fiction novels ever written. Herbert's world-building is unmatched, and the political intrigue combined with ecological themes makes it feel incredibly relevant even today.

The spice melange as a metaphor for oil/resources is brilliant. The Fremen culture and their adaptation to Arrakis is fascinating.

Paul's transformation from reluctant hero to tyrant is a masterclass in subverting the chosen one narrative.

## Characters
- **Paul Atreides**: Complex protagonist who becomes what he feared
- **Lady Jessica**: Powerful Bene Gesserit, Paul's mother
- **Duke Leto**: Noble and tragic figure
- **Baron Harkonnen**: One of the best villains in sci-fi
- **Stilgar**: Fremen leader, bridge between cultures

## Rating: 5/5
Must-read for anyone interested in science fiction.
